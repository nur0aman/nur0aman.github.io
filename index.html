I'm a Postdoctoral Scholar at Dartmouth College in the Department of Computer Science working with Professor David Kotz and Professor Tim Pierson

I'm currently working on “Internet of Things” and “Smart Home” related technologies to develop new scientific understanding and technologically innovative solutions in the line of security, privacy, heterogeneity, and flexibility. With Internet-connected sensors/actuators, locks, home-appliances, etc., more efforts are required to handle new privacy risks, high profile attacks, and vulnerable and unreliable devices. Moreover, making the solutions and network infrastructure automatic, agile, and modular for accurate and immediate effect on any change to the system is part of my research.

Before joining Dartmouth College, I was a Postdoctoral Research Associate advised by Professor Sudip Misra and Postdoctoral Fellow advised by Professor Rajesh Sundaresan at Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur and Indian Institute of Scinece, Bangalore, respectively.

I completed my PhD on Desgining IEEE 802.11ah-based scalable networks for Internet of Things under the guidance of Dr. Md. Iftekhar Hussain from the Department of Information Technology, North-Eastern Hill University, Shillong, India. I received the B.Tech. and M.Tech. degrees in Information Technology from North-Eastern Hill University, Shillong, India.

My current research interests include Internet of Things, Software-defined Network, and WiFi-based long distance Networks.

I'm a member of IEEE and ACM
